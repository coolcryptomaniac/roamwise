Best Time to Visit Ubud, Indonesia: the Crowd-Smart Guide

Most guides tell you where to go in Ubud. This one tells you when — because the same trip in June and August are two different holidays at two different prices. Below: the month-by-month crowd calendar, honest budgets, a 5-day plan, and the food and hidden gems worth planning around.

🗓️ A 5-day Ubud plan that dodges the crowds

Day 1 — Arrival & first wander

Land, drop bags, and walk the nearest old quarter. End with Nasi Goreng for dinner.

Day 2 — The icons — early

Hit the top sights at opening time (crowds arrive after 10am). Afternoon: Tegallalang Rice Terraces.

Day 3 — The hidden side

Campuhan Ridge Walk — this is the day the trip becomes yours. Try Babi Guling where locals queue.

Day 4 — Day trip or deep dive

Pick one: a nearby excursion, or go deep on the district you liked most. Evenings are for sunset viewpoints.

Day 5 — Slow morning & departure

Coffee, one last market run for gifts, and out. You beat the crowds — most visitors never learn the June secret.

❓ FAQ

What is the best time to visit Ubud?

The sweet spot is May, June, September, October — good weather with crowds well below peak. The single quietest month is June at roughly 30% of peak crowd levels.

What is the most crowded month in Ubud?

August sees peak tourist volume (~78% intensity). Prices rise and queues lengthen — book far ahead or shift your dates.

How much does a week in Ubud cost?

Indicative per-person weekly budgets: backpacker ~$600, mid-range ~$1100, luxury ~$2500. Flights, stay and food make up most of it.

Do Indians need a visa for Indonesia?

Free Visa on Arrival (Free 30 days) for stays up to 30 days. Extended to 60 days for free — check Imigrasi Indonesia
