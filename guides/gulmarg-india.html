Best Time to Visit Gulmarg: the Month-by-Month Truth

Gulmarg is a monsoon-rhythm destination — the year splits into a lush green season and a dry golden one. The numbers below are real — monthly weather computed from the ERA5 climate archive for Gulmarg's exact coordinates — so this page answers the question most guides dodge: which months are actually worth your leave days?

The short answer: aim for September, October, November, skip February if you can, and read the packing notes before you book. Post-monsoon months are the secret: everything is green, waterfalls are full, and crowds haven't returned.

❓ FAQ

What is the best time to visit Gulmarg?

September, October, November score highest on our weather-comfort index — daytime around 19.7°C with manageable rainfall. Post-monsoon months are the secret: everything is green, waterfalls are full, and crowds haven't returned.

Which month should I avoid in Gulmarg?

February is the toughest window (-0.9°C days, ~113mm rain). Peak monsoon weeks can disrupt transport and close trails — build slack into plans.

What should I pack for Gulmarg?

Pack a compact umbrella or poncho, quick-dry layers, and waterproof covers for electronics.
