Most guides tell you where to go in Tbilisi. This one tells you when — because the same trip in January and July are two different holidays at two different prices. Below: the month-by-month crowd calendar, honest budgets, a 5-day plan, and the food and hidden gems worth planning around.

🗓️ A 5-day Tbilisi plan that dodges the crowds

Day 1 — Arrival & first wander

Land, drop bags, and walk the nearest old quarter. End with Khinkali for dinner.

Day 2 — The icons — early

Hit the top sights at opening time (crowds arrive after 10am). Afternoon: Kazbegi National Park Gergeti Trinity Church.

Day 3 — The hidden side

Vardzia Cave Monastery — this is the day the trip becomes yours. Try Khachapuri where locals queue.

Day 4 — Day trip or deep dive

Pick one: a nearby excursion, or go deep on the district you liked most. Evenings are for sunset viewpoints.

Day 5 — Slow morning & departure

Coffee, one last market run for gifts, and out. You beat the crowds — most visitors never learn the January secret.

❓ FAQ

What is the best time to visit Tbilisi?

The sweet spot is April, May, June, September, October — good weather with crowds well below peak. The single quietest month is January at roughly 20% of peak crowd levels.

What is the most crowded month in Tbilisi?

July sees peak tourist volume (~65% intensity). Prices rise and queues lengthen — book far ahead or shift your dates.

How much does a week in Tbilisi cost?

Indicative per-person weekly budgets: backpacker ~$600, mid-range ~$1000, luxury ~$2200. Flights, stay and food make up most of it.

Do Indians need a visa for Georgia?
