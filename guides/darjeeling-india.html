Best Time to Visit Darjeeling: the Month-by-Month Truth

Darjeeling is a monsoon-rhythm destination — the year splits into a lush green season and a dry golden one. The numbers below are real — monthly weather computed from the ERA5 climate archive for Darjeeling's exact coordinates — so this page answers the question most guides dodge: which months are actually worth your leave days?

The short answer: aim for February, April, December, skip June if you can, and read the packing notes before you book. Post-monsoon months are the secret: everything is green, waterfalls are full, and crowds haven't returned.

🎒 What to pack for Darjeeling

Pack a compact umbrella or poncho, quick-dry layers, and waterproof covers for electronics. Footwear that survives wet streets earns its space twice over.

🗓️ How to plan the days

Give Darjeeling 3–5 focused days. Mornings for the headline sights (crowds arrive after 10), afternoons for neighbourhoods, evenings for food. ❓ FAQ

What is the best time to visit Darjeeling?

February, April, December score highest on our weather-comfort index — daytime around 10.9°C with manageable rainfall. Post-monsoon months are the secret: everything is green, waterfalls are full, and crowds haven't returned.

Which month should I avoid in Darjeeling?

June is the toughest window (19.8°C days, ~449mm rain). Peak monsoon weeks can disrupt transport and close trails — build slack into plans.

What should I pack for Darjeeling?

Pack a compact umbrella or poncho, quick-dry layers, and waterproof covers for electronics.
