Best Time to Visit Leh: the Month-by-Month Truth

Leh is a mountain-climate destination where the season you pick changes the entire experience. The numbers below are real — monthly weather computed from the ERA5 climate archive for Leh's exact coordinates — so this page answers the question most guides dodge: which months are actually worth your leave days?

The short answer: aim for July, September, October, skip February if you can, and read the packing notes before you book. Shoulder months give you snow-capped views with open roads — the photographer's window.

🎒 What to pack for Leh

Pack proper layers — a warm jacket even in shoulder months, sunscreen for altitude, and broken-in shoes. Altitude amplifies sun and cold at once — respect both.

🗓️ How to plan the days

Give Leh 3–5 focused days. Mornings for the headline sights (crowds arrive after 10), afternoons for neighbourhoods, evenings for food. ❓ FAQ

What is the best time to visit Leh?

July, September, October score highest on our weather-comfort index — daytime around 24.5°C with manageable rainfall. Shoulder months give you snow-capped views with open roads — the photographer's window.

Which month should I avoid in Leh?

February is the toughest window (-5.6°C days, ~33mm rain). Winter access can close passes and roads; always check conditions before booking.

What should I pack for Leh?

Pack proper layers — a warm jacket even in shoulder months, sunscreen for altitude, and broken-in shoes.

How many days do I need in Leh?
